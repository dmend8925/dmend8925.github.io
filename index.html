Dylan Mendoza

PhD Student | Department of Physics and Astronomy | University of Rochester

Originally from Colorado, I completed my undergraduate studies at the University of Northern Colorado, where I received a B.S. in Physics and a B.A. in Philosophy. During my time at UNC, my research spanned topics from biophysics to exoplanets. I often incorporate my physics training into my work in philosophy, questioning the epistemic limits of our theories. While I still enjoy thinking about how we ought to do science, my time at Rochester has shifted my focus to brown dwarfs and exoplanets; specifically, how we can best characterize their atmospheres in order to understand their formation history and future.
